About the Author
Chong Hyon-Jong (1939-2021) was a South Korean poet who is known for his work on the themes of love, loss, and loneliness. He was born in Seoul, South Korea, in 1939. He studied philosophy at Yonsei University, and he began publishing his poems in the early 1960s.

Chong's poetry is characterized by its simple and direct language. He often uses everyday objects and experiences to explore complex emotions. His poems are often about the pain of loss and the difficulty of finding love. However, they also express a deep hope and optimism.

Chong's poetry has been translated into many languages, and he is considered one of the most important poets of South Korea. He received numerous awards for his work, including the Kim Soo-young Literary Award in 1971 and the Dong-in Literary Award in 1977.

Chong died in 2021 at the age of 82. He is survived by his wife and two children.
